ENTITY RESOLUTION AND CLIENT HIERARCHY GUIDE (MOCK)

Purpose
Explains how client legal entities are matched and linked to build an
accurate client hierarchy.

Matching Rules
- Exact match on regulatory identifier (for example, an LEI or company
  registration number) is treated as a confirmed match.
- Fuzzy match on legal name plus country of incorporation requires
  analyst confirmation before the entities are merged.
- Entities with no regulatory identifier and a low name-similarity score
  are treated as distinct clients.

Hierarchy Modelling
- Each client entity records its immediate parent, if any.
- Ultimate parent and beneficial ownership chains are derived by walking
  the parent links until no further parent is found.
- Affiliates that share an ultimate parent but have no direct ownership
  link are tagged as related parties, not consolidated entities.

Data Quality
Duplicate entity records are the most common data-quality issue during
onboarding. Any duplicate detected after onboarding must be merged and
the merge decision recorded for audit purposes.
