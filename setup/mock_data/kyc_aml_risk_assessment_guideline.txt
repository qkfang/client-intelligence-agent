KYC / AML RISK ASSESSMENT GUIDELINE (MOCK)

Purpose
Provides the criteria used to assess money-laundering and sanctions risk
for a client during onboarding and periodic review.

Risk Factors
- Jurisdiction risk: clients incorporated in high-risk or sanctioned
  jurisdictions receive an automatic uplift in risk rating.
- Ownership complexity: layered or opaque ownership structures increase
  risk and require enhanced due diligence.
- Product risk: complex derivatives and cross-border payments are rated
  higher risk than vanilla cash products.
- Adverse media: confirmed adverse media hits require a compliance
  review before the case can proceed.

Risk Ratings
- Low: standard due diligence, reviewed every 3 years.
- Medium: enhanced due diligence, reviewed annually.
- High: enhanced due diligence with compliance sign-off, reviewed every
  6 months.

Sanctions and PEP Screening
Every client and its beneficial owners are screened against sanctions and
politically exposed persons (PEP) lists at onboarding and on every
periodic review. Any potential match must be dispositioned by a
compliance analyst before the case can move forward.
